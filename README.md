
<h2>This project provides a comprehensive data analysis of Nobel Prize laureates from 1901 to the present day. Using a dataset containing information on nearly 1,000 winners, it explores the patterns and trends behind one of the world's most prestigious honors.</h2>

<h2>Core Concepts</h2>
<ul>Historical Trends: Analyzing the evolution of Nobel Prizes across various categories including Physics, Chemistry, Literature, Peace, Economics, and Physiology or Medicine.</ul>

<ul>Demographic Insights: Investigating laureate backgrounds, such as the distribution of prizes by gender and the common birthplaces of winners</ul>

<ul>Data Cleaning & Processing: Utilizing Python and pandas to handle missing values, convert data types (like birth dates and years), and calculate new metrics such as prize share percentages.</ul>

<ul>Visual Exploration: Leveraging visualization libraries like Plotly, Seaborn, and Matplotlib to create interactive charts, including donut charts for gender representation and geographical distribution maps.</ul>

<h2>Key Questions Addressed</h2>

<ul>What global patterns exist among Nobel laureates?</ul>
<ul>How has the percentage of female winners changed over time?</ul>
<ul>Which organizations or countries have conferred the most benefit to humankind according to Nobel’s legacy?</ul>
